1. The Core Tools: Your "Bandar" Radar
To track the big players, you cannot just look at the price chart. You must look at the data behind the trades. In the Indonesian market, our greatest weapon is the Broker Summary (Broksum).

Broker Summary: This shows you exactly which brokerages are buying and selling a specific stock at the end of the day.

Foreign Flow: Often, foreign institutional investors act as massive market makers. Tracking Net Foreign Buy and Net Foreign Sell is crucial for blue-chip stocks.

Volume: Price can be manipulated easily with small volume. Volume is the actual money. It is the footprint the Bandar cannot erase.

2. The Four Phases of Bandarmology
To track the Bandar, you must know what game they are currently playing. Every stock cycle goes through four distinct phases manipulated by the big players:

Phase A: Accumulation (The Stealth Phase)
This is where the Bandar collects shares at wholesale prices.

The Price Action: The price usually moves sideways or slowly drifts downwards. It looks boring. Retail investors are frustrated and selling out of boredom or fear.

The Broksum Footprint: Look for 1 to 3 big brokers consistently placing massive Net Buys over a period of weeks or months, while the sellers are fragmented across dozens of retail brokers.

The Tactic: They buy quietly. If they buy all at once, the price will spike. They buy, let the price drop, and buy again.

Phase B: Mark-Up (The Participation Phase)
The Bandar has enough inventory. Now, they need the price to go up so they can sell it later at a premium.

The Price Action: The stock breaks out of its sideways trend. It starts making higher highs.

The Broksum Footprint: The Bandar uses their "right hand" to buy from their "left hand" (crossing) to create artificial volume and paint a beautiful technical chart.

The Tactic: Good news and rumors suddenly appear in the media. Technical analysts trigger "Buy" signals. Retail investors start rushing in because of FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out).

Phase C: Distribution (The Unloading Phase)
This is the most dangerous phase for retail investors. The Bandar is taking their profits.

The Price Action: The price might be at its peak, often moving violently up and down or stalling completely despite high volume. The news is overwhelmingly positive.

The Broksum Footprint: Here is the trap! The Top 1-3 brokers are now massive Net Sellers. Who is buying? Dozens of different retail brokers. The wealth is being transferred from the smart money to the dumb money.

The Tactic: They sell into the buying power of the retail crowd. As long as retail keeps buying the "good news," the Bandar keeps feeding them shares.

Phase D: Mark-Down (The Despair Phase)
The Bandar is completely out of the stock. Without their buying support, gravity takes over.

The Price Action: The price collapses. Retail investors are trapped at the top and start panic selling.

The Tactic: The Bandar sits back with their cash, waiting for the price to hit rock bottom so they can begin Phase A (Accumulation) all over again.

3. Step-by-Step: How to Analyze the Broker Summary
When you open your trading app at the end of the day, here is exactly how you read the Broksum to spot accumulation or distribution:

Look at the Top Buyers vs. Top Sellers: Compare the top 3 or top 5 buyers against the top 3 or top 5 sellers.

Calculate the Concentration: * If the Top 3 Buyers bought 500,000 lots, but the Top 3 Sellers only sold 200,000 lots, that means buying is concentrated and selling is fragmented. This is Accumulation. Big money is absorbing the shares.

If the Top 3 Sellers sold 500,000 lots, but the Top 3 Buyers only bought 200,000 lots, selling is concentrated and buying is fragmented. This is Distribution. The Bandar is dumping shares onto the retail crowd.

Identify the Brokers: Learn which broker codes are typically used by institutions/foreign funds (e.g., in Indonesia, codes like RX, ZP, CS, AK, BK) versus which ones are favored by retail traders (e.g., YP, PD, NI, CC). If institutional brokers are selling to retail brokers, run away.

Check Average Price: Look at the average price the Top Buyer accumulated their shares. If the current market price is still very close to the Bandar's average price, the risk is relatively low. If the price is already 50% above their average, you are risking buying at the top of their Mark-Up phase.

Golden Rule: Never buy a stock purely because it is going up. Buy a stock because you have verified through the Broker Summary that the big money is actively accumulating it. Protect your capital, respect the whale, and don't be the liquidity for their exit.
